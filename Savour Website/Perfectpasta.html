5 Tips for Perfect Pasta

Pasta is a loved dish worldwide, but achieving that perfect al dente texture and rich flavour takes a bit of know-how. Whether you’re a beginner or a seasoned cook, these five tips will elevate your pasta-making skills.

  1. Use Plenty of Salted Water

    Salt your water generously to flavour the pasta from within. A good rule of thumb is about 1 tablespoon of salt per litre of water. The water should taste like the sea!

  2. Don't Add Oil to the Water

    Adding oil to the water will prevent the sauce from sticking to the pasta. Instead, stir the pasta occasionally to prevent sticking.

  3. Cook Until Just Al Dante

    Check your pasta a minute or two before the package instructions say it's done. Pasta will continue to cook slightly after draining, especially if you toss it with a hot sauce.

  4. Save Some Pasta Water

    Before draining your pasta, reserve about a cup of the starchy cooking water. Adding this to your sauce can help it cling to the pasta and create a silky, cohesive dish.

  5. Toss Pasta with the Sauce Immediately

    Don't let your pasta sit around after draining. Toss it with your sauce immediately to ensure that the pasta absorbs the flavours and the sauce clings to the noodles.

With these simple yet effective tips, you’ll be serving up pasta dishes that taste like they’re straight from an Italian kitchen. Buon appetito!
